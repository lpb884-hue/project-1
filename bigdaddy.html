Why I Like It

At #8, I have Big Daddy. Most Adam Sandler movies have static characters, or characters who experience little to no personal growth over the story. Big Daddy is somewhat different though, because Sandlers character Sonny is a guy who struggles with repsonsibility and has to learn how to raise a kid after adopting one. This movie is also more emotionally deep than most of his movies, and I like that it doesn't follow the Sandler blueprint in that way. It also took me way to long in my life to learn that the kid in this movie is played by twins.
